Utility-Scale Ground-Mounted Systems

Utility-scale solar energy has become the lowest cost electricity in many parts of the world in ideal environmental conditions, even with minor patronage from governments. In Sri Lanka, authorities have set a cap of LKR 18.37 as Feed in Tariff. This rate is attractive for systems in the range of 10 megawatts, and would be tight for smaller-scale systems. In the long term, this would be a luxury for the utility service provider, as this tariff is fixed without any escalation for 20 years despite inflation.
